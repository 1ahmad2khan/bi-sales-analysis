# Retail Sales Analysis — Business Report

Overview

This report summarises findings from an analysis of retail transactional 
data covering 9,994 orders across four years (2015–2018). The analysis 
examines revenue trends, category and regional performance, and top 
performing products to support strategic decision-making.

Key Findings

1. Revenue is growing year on year

Sales have increased consistently across all four years. 2018 is tracking 
notably higher than previous years, suggesting the business is in a healthy 
growth phase. This trend should be monitored to identify what is driving 
the growth and whether it can be sustained.

2. Sales follow a predictable seasonal pattern

September and November are consistently the strongest months across all 
years, whilst February is persistently the weakest. This pattern is stable 
enough to plan around — stock levels, staffing and marketing spend should 
reflect this seasonality rather than treating each month equally.

3. Technology drives the most revenue

Technology is the top performing category at approximately $830,000 in 
total sales, followed by Furniture and Office Supplies at around $730,000 
and $705,000 respectively. Whilst the categories are relatively balanced, 
Technology's lead is consistent and worth protecting.

4. The South region is significantly underperforming

The West and East regions lead with approximately $710,000 and $670,000 
in sales respectively. The South region trails considerably at around 
$390,000 — a gap of over $300,000 compared to the West. This warrants 
further investigation to determine whether the gap is due to market size, 
competition, or gaps in sales coverage.

5. A small number of products drive disproportionate revenue

The Canon imageCLASS 2200 Advanced Copier alone accounts for approximately 
$61,000 in sales — nearly double the second highest product. Seven of the 
top ten products are office or technology equipment, reinforcing the 
strength of the Technology category.


Recommendations

- *Seasonal planning: Align procurement, marketing and staffing with the 
September and November peaks identified in the data.

- South region review: Conduct a deeper diagnostic of the South region 
to understand the performance gap and identify corrective actions.

- Technology investment: Given consistent leadership across categories 
and top products, continued investment in the Technology category is 
justified.

- Product portfolio: Monitor dependency on a small number of high-value 
products. Diversifying the top performers reduces revenue risk.


Methodology

Data was analysed using Python (Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn) and SQL. 
All figures are based on total sales values from the raw transactional 
dataset. Visualisations are available in the dashboard folder.
